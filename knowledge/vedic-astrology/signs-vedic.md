# Vedic Rashi (Signs)

Vedic astrology uses the sidereal zodiac (fixed stars), offset from the tropical zodiac by ~24 degrees (Lahiri ayanamsa). This means Vedic sign placements differ from Western.

**Mesha (Aries)** — Ruled by Mars. Fiery, courageous, pioneering, impulsive. First rashi.
**Vrishabha (Taurus)** — Ruled by Venus. Stable, artistic, sensual, stubborn.
**Mithuna (Gemini)** — Ruled by Mercury. Intellectual, dual-natured, communicative.
**Karka (Cancer)** — Ruled by Moon. Nurturing, emotional, home-loving, intuitive.
**Simha (Leo)** — Ruled by Sun. Royal, generous, dramatic, powerful.
**Kanya (Virgo)** — Ruled by Mercury. Analytical, healing, precise, critical.
**Tula (Libra)** — Ruled by Venus. Balanced, diplomatic, artistic, indecisive.
**Vrishchika (Scorpio)** — Ruled by Mars. Secretive, intense, transformative, occult.
**Dhanu (Sagittarius)** — Ruled by Jupiter. Philosophical, adventurous, spiritual, optimistic.
**Makara (Capricorn)** — Ruled by Saturn. Ambitious, disciplined, practical, reserved.
**Kumbha (Aquarius)** — Ruled by Saturn. Humanitarian, eccentric, detached, reforming.
**Meena (Pisces)** — Ruled by Jupiter. Spiritual, empathic, dreamy, moksha-oriented.

## Key Differences from Western Astrology
- Lagna (Ascendant) is given MORE weight than Sun sign in Vedic interpretation
- Moon sign is critically important — governs mind, mother, emotions
- Sun sign is secondary (ego, father, authority)
- Yogas (planetary combinations) create special effects beyond sign placement
- Retrograde planets are considered stronger in Vedic astrology
