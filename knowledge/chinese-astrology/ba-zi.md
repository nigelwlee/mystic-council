# Ba Zi: Four Pillars of Destiny

Ba Zi (八字, "Eight Characters") charts four pillars — Year, Month, Day, Hour — each containing a Heavenly Stem and Earthly Branch. Together they reveal the blueprint of a person's destiny.

## The Four Pillars

**Year Pillar** — Ancestral heritage, early life, social reputation. How the world sees you.
**Month Pillar** — Career, parents, formative years (ages ~16-30). The core productive phase.
**Day Pillar** — The self (Day Stem is the "Day Master"), relationships, marriage.
**Hour Pillar** — Children, later life, hidden desires, subconscious nature.

## The Day Master
The Heavenly Stem of the Day Pillar is the Day Master — the most important element. It represents the self and is analyzed in relation to all other elements to determine:
- **Favorable elements**: strengthen and support the Day Master
- **Unfavorable elements**: weaken or exhaust the Day Master

## Ten Heavenly Stems
Jiǎ (Yang Wood), Yǐ (Yin Wood), Bǐng (Yang Fire), Dīng (Yin Fire),
Wù (Yang Earth), Jǐ (Yin Earth), Gēng (Yang Metal), Xīn (Yin Metal),
Rén (Yang Water), Guǐ (Yin Water)

## Twelve Earthly Branches
Each branch contains hidden stems. Example: Zǐ (Rat) contains hidden Guǐ Water.

## Reading the Chart
1. Identify element balance — which elements are strong/weak?
2. Determine Day Master's strength
3. Find favorable (useful gods) and unfavorable elements
4. Read pillar relationships: clashes, combinations, harms
5. Apply 10-year luck pillars (大運) flowing from birth

## Key Concepts
- **Clashing branches** (e.g., Rat vs. Horse, Ox vs. Goat): disruption and change
- **Combining stems** (e.g., Jiǎ+Jǐ = Earth transformation): merging energies
- **Six harmonies**: compatible branches that enhance each other
