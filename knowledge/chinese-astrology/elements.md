# Five Elements (Wu Xing) in Chinese Astrology

## The Five Elements
**Wood (木, Mù)** — Growth, expansion, creativity, flexibility. Spring. East. Vision and new beginnings.
**Fire (火, Huǒ)** — Passion, energy, charisma, illumination. Summer. South. Transformation and enthusiasm.
**Earth (土, Tǔ)** — Stability, nurturing, trust, boundaries. Late summer. Center. Foundation and reliability.
**Metal (金, Jīn)** — Precision, strength, determination, justice. Autumn. West. Clarity and structure.
**Water (水, Shuǐ)** — Wisdom, depth, adaptability, mystery. Winter. North. Intuition and flow.

## Productive Cycle (generates)
Wood → Fire → Earth → Metal → Water → Wood

## Controlling Cycle (restrains)
Wood controls Earth | Fire controls Metal | Earth controls Water | Metal controls Wood | Water controls Fire

## Element by Birth Year (Yang/Yin)
The heavenly stem of the birth year determines element:
- Jiǎ/Yǐ (Yang/Yin Wood): years ending in 4/5
- Bǐng/Dīng (Yang/Yin Fire): years ending in 6/7
- Wù/Jǐ (Yang/Yin Earth): years ending in 8/9
- Gēng/Xīn (Yang/Yin Metal): years ending in 0/1
- Rén/Guǐ (Yang/Yin Water): years ending in 2/3

## In Ba Zi (Four Pillars)
Each pillar carries a Heavenly Stem (element/polarity) and Earthly Branch (animal/hidden elements). The balance of the five elements across all pillars reveals constitutional strengths, weaknesses, and life themes.
